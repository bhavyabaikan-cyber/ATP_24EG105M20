Down with the state

We advocate for a significant reduction in the size and scope of government power. Centralized authority often leads to inefficiency, corruption, and the erosion of individual rights. By dismantling bureaucratic overreach and returning power to the people, we can foster a society based on voluntary cooperation rather than state-mandated force.

But what about the roads?

This classic question highlights the common fear that essential services can only be provided by the state. However, history and modern innovation show that private enterprise and community initiatives are often more efficient at building infrastructure. When the market is allowed to solve problems, we get better results at a lower cost without government monopolies.

Is taxation theft?

If an individual takes your money without your consent, it is called theft. When the state does it under the threat of force, the principle remains the same. We believe that people should have full control over the fruits of their labor. A system built on voluntary contributions and fee-for-service models is a more moral and just alternative to compulsory taxation.

So, what you're saying is...?

What we are saying is that a society built on liberty, property rights, and non-aggression is not just a dream—it's a necessity for human flourishing. We believe in a world where you are free to pursue your own happiness, keep what you earn, and interact with others peacefully and voluntarily.
